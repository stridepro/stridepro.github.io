Swimmer Name: Johnny Swimmer

Date of evaluation: 1/11/2016

Concern: Evaluation of track start, butterfly and open turn

Submission: Kingston has submitted two videos, one of a start from the blocks into a breaststroke breakout and also a video of a 25 yard butterfly with a side view and open butterfly turn.

Evaluation: The most important tips have been put in bold print.

Start:

1.     Bring your head closer to your chest in order to position yourself better on the block (think about almost touching your chin to your chest) This will also bring your hips up to give you more power when you push off the block. (video link below)

2.     Your dominant leg is in the front on a track start and you want most of your weight to be on your that leg because it will give you the most power when you push off.

3.     You did a great job getting your hands into a streamline before entering the water. When you enter the water try pointing those hands to where you want to enter the water, a little further from the wall.

Butterfly

Streamline: You did a good job getting your hands together on the streamline.

1.     Try to stay underwater for longer, try 4 butterfly kicks before coming to the surface of the water.

Breathing:

1.     If you feel yourself getting tired quickly on butterfly, try breathing 2 strokes in a row then not breathing for the third stroke.

1.     For example- breath, breath, no breath, breath, breath, no breath. This breathing pattern may help you from fatiguing as quickly.

Head Position: You do a good job of getting your head back inline with your body after you breathe.

1.     When you are taking your breath, try to look more at the bottom of the pool rather than at the opposite end of the pool.

Kick:

1.     The most important part of butterfly kick is to keep your legs together, do not let them separate into a flutterkick, this will cause a disqualification and nobody wants that!!

1.     this happened twice in your submission and I believe it was caused by tiredness. The more you practice your butterfly and kick the more comfortable and easy it will become.

2.     There are 2 kicks for each pull in butterfly. One small kick when your arms exit the water and one BIG kick when your hands enter the water. (video link below)

Turn:

1.     When touching the wall with 2 hands, try to touch the wall just below the water.

1.     You don't want to grab onto the wall, because then you will automatically pull yourself up and into the wall instead of leaving the wall as quickly as possible.

2.     You do a good job of bringing your left elbow to your ribs first.

1.     Now try to get your feet onto the wall at the same time you bring your right hang to your ear. (Video link below focus on 0:46-1:20 in the turn video)

1.     One of your goals should be to get your feet onto the wall as fast as you can after touching it with your hands.
